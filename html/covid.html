COVID-19

Basics

What It Is

The Coronavirus is a family of viruses that affect the respiratory system. There are hundreds of strains, but only seven of them affect humans. The new strain of the virus, SARS-CoV or COVID-19, first appeared in Wuhan China in December 2019 and then took its hold on the rest of the world. This virus is classified as a pandemic. According to the World Health Organization (WHO), a pandemic is a “worldwide spread of a new disease.” As of now, only 12 countries in the entire world have no report of Covid-19 cases.

How It Spreads

This virus spreads from person-to-person. Within close contact, it spreads easily between people. Through respiratory droplets from an infected individual (like coughing, sneezing, or even talking), the virus is able to pass on to others when these droplets are inhaled or landing on the mouths and noses of those nearby. It’s also believed to spread through touching an infected surface, and then using that same hand to touch areas of the face. Not-So-Fun Fact: Even asymptomatic people can spread this disease. Meaning, that there are people that don’t even have noticeable symptoms of COVID-19 that can still carry and spread it.

Symptoms

  • Shortness of Breath
  • Unexplained loss of taste or smell
  • Cough
  • Muscle Aches
  • Sore Throat
  • Fever
  • Diarrhea
  • Headache

How To Stay Safe

  • Social Distance: Stay more than six feet apart when coming into contact with people
  • Wear a Mask: Whenever you’re out in public, please be sure to wear any sort of protective cloth that covers both your mouth AND nose.
  • Wash Your Hands: Thoroughly scrub and rinse your hands with warm water and soap. Don’t just do it after you use the toilet. Do it as soon as you come home, before eating food, after coughing/sneezing/blowing your nose, and following a touch to an unsanitary surface (to only name a few instances).
  • Disinfect surfaces: With gloves on, we recommend that you constantly clean frequently-touched surfaces like cellphones, faucets, keys, kitchen counter, door knobs, etc.
